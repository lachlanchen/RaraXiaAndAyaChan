# Robot, Drone, And AR Lab

This series teaches robotics, drones, dexterous hands, AR glasses, and physical
AI through funny physical stories. It is the natural continuation after `Chip
Factory Adventures`: once the chips are built, the characters ask what those
chips can do in the real world.

## Series Promise

Lala Xia, Aya Chan, Sasa Kun, and LazyingArtRobot enter a lab where machines must
learn to see, touch, fly, balance, and understand space. Every technology becomes
a playful scene: a drone refuses to fly through a messy room, a robot hand drops
a strawberry too gently, AR glasses place a word-card in the wrong dimension,
and LazyingArtRobot tries to follow safety rules while Sasa turns everything into
a race.

## Main Characters

- Lala Xia: thinks robot skills are judged by whether they can deliver food.
- Aya Chan: explains the difference between seeing, understanding, and acting.
- Sasa Kun: volunteers as test pilot before reading the safety card.
- LazyingArtRobot: lab assistant and recurring robot character; proud of
  calibration, hates unplanned bamboo experiments.
- Words Card: physical learning widget; can appear as a real card or as an AR
  anchored object shown through LightMind AI glasses.

## Core Concepts

- Perception: sensing and interpreting the world.
- Planning: choosing a safe action path.
- Control: turning a plan into motion.
- SLAM: mapping while locating yourself.
- Grasping: holding objects with force and touch feedback.
- Haptics: sensing touch and force.
- Latency: delay between command, sensing, and response.
- Embodied AI: intelligence that must act in the physical world.
- Simulation: practicing safely before acting in reality.
- Spatial computing: digital objects placed in physical space.

## Story Method

Each episode should make the concept visible:

- Perception becomes "the robot sees a strawberry but thinks it is a red button."
- Planning becomes "the drone draws three paths, and only one avoids the soup."
- Control becomes "Sasa's bamboo wobbles because the controller overreacts."
- Haptics becomes "the robot hand learns not to crush a lychee."
- Latency becomes "the AR arrow arrives late and sends Lala Xia into a cushion."
- SLAM becomes "the drone maps the room while trying not to chase its own shadow."

## Season 1 Arc: From Seeing To Helping

### Episode 01: The Robot That Took Metaphors Literally

Concept: embodied AI.

LazyingArtRobot hears Lala Xia say, "Give me a hand," and detaches a tool-hand
from the shelf instead of helping. Aya explains that a robot needs language,
perception, and action together. The words card shows `embodied / 身体化`.

Hook: the robot tries to "lend a hand" to a drone.

### Episode 02: Drone Sees The Soup

Concept: obstacle sensing.

A tiny drone must fly across a lab table without hitting a bowl of soup. Sasa
cheers, "Go straight!" The drone stops because its sensors see the steam and
the bowl. Aya explains that obstacle sensing is useful, but not magic.

Hook: the drone starts drawing a map in the air.

### Episode 03: The Room That Drew Itself

Concept: SLAM.

The drone flies slowly and draws a glowing map while also locating itself. Lala
Xia asks if the map includes snack shelves. LazyingArtRobot says the map is for
navigation, then quietly marks the snack shelf anyway.

Hook: the map shows a door that was not there before.

### Episode 04: Strawberry Grip Test

Concept: dexterous grasping and tactile sensing.

A robot hand must pick up a strawberry without crushing it. Sasa says "hold it
tightly," and the strawberry squeaks. Aya uses the words card: `haptics /
触覚 / しょっかく`. The robot learns to feel pressure.

Hook: the strawberry opens like a tiny red key.

### Episode 05: The Latency Cushion

Concept: latency.

AR glasses show an arrow telling Lala Xia where to step. The arrow is delayed,
so he steps into a cushion pile. Aya explains latency: the slower the response,
the less real the digital object feels.

Hook: the cushion pile hides an old AR calibration card.

### Episode 06: Robot Learns In A Dream Room

Concept: simulation.

LazyingArtRobot enters a simulation where it can practice catching falling
objects without breaking real ones. Sasa joins the simulation and turns it into a
roller-coaster. Aya says simulation is for safe training before real deployment.

Hook: the simulation generates a challenge the robot never saw before.

### Episode 07: The Drone That Followed Too Well

Concept: tracking.

The drone uses active tracking to follow Sasa, but Sasa runs around a table,
under a chair, and behind a plant. The drone stops safely instead of crashing.
Lala Xia says, "A good follower knows when not to follow."

Hook: the plant moves by itself.

### Episode 08: Robot Hand Versus Slippery Lychee

Concept: friction and tactile feedback.

Aya Chan gives the robot hand a lychee. It is round and slippery. Lala Xia is
offended that the robot gets lychee first. The robot learns to adjust grip force
using tactile feedback.

Hook: the lychee rolls into an AR portal.

### Episode 09: LightMind AR Word Card

Concept: spatial anchoring.

Through LightMind AI glasses, a word-card appears stuck to the table. Sasa moves
his head and expects the card to follow, but it stays anchored. Aya explains that
spatial computing places digital content in the physical world.

Hook: the card flips and reveals a hidden robot route.

### Episode 10: The Safety Button Race

Concept: safety and control limits.

Sasa challenges the robot to a race. The robot refuses to move until it checks
people, obstacles, and emergency stops. Lala Xia complains that the robot is too
slow, but the safety check prevents a crash.

Hook: the lab door opens into an outdoor drone field.

### Episode 11: Windy Drone Field

Concept: control under disturbance.

Wind pushes the drone sideways. Sasa says the drone is "being dramatic." Aya
explains that control systems must correct for disturbances. The robot uses the
word-card as a tiny wind flag.

Hook: the wind carries the card toward a robot warehouse.

### Episode 12: Warehouse Helper

Concept: practical humanoid robot tasks.

In a small warehouse, LazyingArtRobot learns that helping is not just looking
human. It must carry, sort, avoid people, recover from mistakes, and ask for help
when uncertain.

Hook: a shelf label says the next world is `Chip Factory Adventures Season 2`.

## Prompt Guardrails

- Keep the word card as a physical or clearly anchored AR object, not subtitles.
- Show safety as a story action, not a warning paragraph.
- Do not claim robots or drones are perfect; make limitations funny and useful.
- Keep LightMind AI glasses visible when AR or spatial computing is central.
- Use LazyingArtRobot as a recurring character, not a generic robot.
