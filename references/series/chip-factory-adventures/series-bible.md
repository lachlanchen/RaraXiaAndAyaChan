# Chip Factory Adventures

This is a LALACHAN educational comedy series about chips, semiconductor
manufacturing, AI accelerators, and computer architecture. It should teach real
technology through story, not lectures.

## Series Promise

Lala Xia, Aya Chan, Sasa Kun, and LazyingArtRobot visit a magical chip factory
where every chip concept becomes a physical puzzle. The factory is cute and
cinematic: glowing wafers, cleanroom tunnels, tiny transistor cities, memory
bridges, packaging elevators, and robot inspectors. Each episode explains one
technical concept with a funny misunderstanding and a small visual payoff.

## Main Characters

- Lala Xia: believes every technical term is food until proven otherwise.
- Aya Chan: careful translator of concepts; uses the handmade notebook as a lab
  log and map.
- Sasa Kun: presses the wrong button, but often triggers the necessary test mode.
- LazyingArtRobot: precise factory guide; speaks like a safety checklist but is
  secretly proud when the trio understands a concept.
- Words Card: physical e-ink study widget that displays one keyword per episode.

## Teaching Method

Each episode should follow this path:

1. Turn a technology into a visible object.
2. Let Lala Xia or Sasa Kun misunderstand the term.
3. Let the robot perform a tiny demonstration.
4. Let Aya Chan give one clear sentence.
5. Use the concept to solve the plot.

Do not explain too many concepts in one video. One episode, one concept.

## Visual Language

- Cleanroom corridors as glowing white mazes.
- Silicon wafers as round moonlike lakes.
- Lithography masks as magical stencils.
- Yield as green/gray die squares after testing.
- Packaging as tiny bridges connecting chiplets.
- Cache as a fast snack drawer close to the chef.
- GPU as many tiny workers doing the same task together.
- NPU as a specialist that recognizes patterns quickly.
- ISA as a contract language between software and silicon.
- RISC-V as an open recipe book; Arm as a licensed instruction family.

## Season 1 Arc: From Sand To Smart Chip

### Episode 01: The Wafer Moon

Concept: wafer.

The trio enters a cleanroom where a giant round silicon wafer floats like a small
moon. Lala Xia thinks it is a cookie tray. LazyingArtRobot panics and says,
"Do not bite the wafer." Aya Chan explains that chips are made on a silicon
wafer before being cut apart. The words card shows `wafer / ウェハー`.

Hook: one square on the wafer blinks red.

### Episode 02: The Mask That Draws Light

Concept: lithography mask.

A stencil-like mask projects tiny patterns onto the wafer. Sasa Kun holds it
upside down and the transistor streets become a noodle maze. The robot shows that
light transfers patterns. Aya writes "mask means pattern guide" in the notebook.

Hook: the wrong pattern creates a tiny traffic jam inside the chip city.

### Episode 03: Yield Is Not Yielding Snacks

Concept: yield.

The factory counts green good chips and gray failed chips. Lala Xia hears
"yield" and politely yields his snack bag to the wafer. Aya explains that yield
means good chips divided by total chips. The words card becomes the scorekeeper.

Hook: the yield drops because Sasa's bamboo left dust on the wafer.

### Episode 04: Dust Is The Cleanroom Monster

Concept: contamination.

A single dust particle appears as a huge fluffy boulder in transistor city. The
robot starts a cleanroom dance: gloves, airflow, filters, and careful movement.
Sasa tries to help by blowing dust away and makes it worse.

Hook: a filter door opens into the packaging floor.

### Episode 05: The Chiplet Bridge

Concept: advanced packaging / chiplets.

Separate chip islands need to talk through tiny bridges. Lala Xia calls them
"snack islands." Aya explains that packaging is how chips connect to each other,
memory, and the board. The robot builds a tiny bridge with Sasa's bamboo.

Hook: one island asks for faster memory.

### Episode 06: The Memory Lunch Line

Concept: memory bandwidth and locality.

Tiny compute workers line up for memory snacks. The line is too long. Aya
creates a close snack drawer called cache. The workers cheer because nearby data
arrives faster.

Hook: a giant GPU hall opens, full of thousands of tiny workers.

### Episode 07: GPU Ant Army

Concept: parallel computing.

The GPU hall has many workers painting identical squares at once. Lala Xia says,
"So the GPU is a thousand tiny chefs making dumplings together?" Aya says that is
surprisingly close. The robot stamps the words card: `parallel / 並列`.

Hook: one worker asks for a tensor core turbo tool.

### Episode 08: Tensor Core Bento Box

Concept: matrix multiplication for AI.

Tiny numbers are arranged like bento boxes. The Tensor Core machine combines many
numbers quickly. Sasa tries to eat the matrix. The robot says, "That is not a
rice grid." Aya explains it is a fast math kitchen for AI.

Hook: the AI model becomes too big for one room.

### Episode 09: NVLink Rope Bridge

Concept: high-speed interconnect.

Several chip buildings need to act like one bigger computer. A glowing bridge
connects them so data can cross quickly. Lala Xia calls it the "group chat rope."
The robot admits this is a useful metaphor.

Hook: the factory asks whether the AI should run in the cloud or on the edge.

### Episode 10: Tiny Edge Brain

Concept: NPU / edge AI.

The trio visits a phone-sized chip that recognizes fruit without asking the
cloud. MediaTek-style edge AI becomes a tiny brain on a skateboard. Aya explains
that an NPU accelerates AI tasks close to the user.

Hook: the tiny brain refuses to identify Lala Xia's mystery snack.

### Episode 11: Arm And RISC-V Recipe Books

Concept: ISA.

Two recipe books appear. One is a polished licensed cookbook; one is an open
recipe notebook that many builders can use. Aya explains that an ISA is the
language software uses to tell hardware what to do.

Hook: Sasa writes an instruction that means "jump," and the robot literally jumps.

### Episode 12: The AI Factory Clock

Concept: manufacturing scale and smart factory.

The factory becomes a living clock: robots move trays, cameras inspect parts,
and AI predicts failures. LazyingArtRobot says the best factory is not just fast;
it notices problems early.

Hook: the clock hands point toward a new lab labeled "Robot, Drone, And AR."
