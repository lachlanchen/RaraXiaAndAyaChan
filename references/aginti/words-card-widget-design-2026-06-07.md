## Product Idea

`Words Card` is a small desktop e-ink learning widget for LALACHAN videos and
real product storytelling. It should feel like a collectible study gadget:
premium enough to buy, simple enough to understand in one glance, and physical
enough to appear naturally inside scenes.

## Physical Design

- Shape: small landscape or portrait card, about postcard size.
- Frame: translucent frosted acrylic, soft rounded corners, visible layered edge.
- Stand: clear acrylic base, slightly angled backward like a desk photo frame.
- Screen: warm-white e-ink surface, no phone-like glow.
- Logo: tiny LazyingArt panda mark or engraved badge centered on the lower bezel.
- Optional accessory: small wafer coin, robot dock, or lab bench magnet beside it.
- Color accents: black, muted red, olive green, and warm gray, matching existing
  word-card references.

## Screen Layout

Use one concept per card:

```text
yield
歩留まり
ぶどまり
good chips / total chips
```

Recommended layout:

- English word large at top.
- Japanese word large in the middle.
- Furigana below the Japanese word.
- Micro-meaning at bottom in small type.
- Optional tiny diagram: green good chip squares divided by total chip squares.

## Story Use

The card should be a real prop:

- LazyingArtRobot slides it onto the lab bench.
- Aya Chan points to it while explaining one sentence.
- Lala Xia misunderstands `yield` as "please give snacks."
- Sasa Kun tries to use it as a cleanroom fan, causing the dust joke.
- The card flashes or flips only as a physical object, not as a subtitle.

## Chip Episode Example

Scene: the trio enters a cleanroom where a wafer looks like a silver moon.

Lala Xia: "Yield? So the chip factory politely gives me snacks?"

LazyingArtRobot holds up the card.

Aya Chan: "不是啦，yield 是歩留まり，就是成功做出来的好芯片比例。"

The wafer shows many tiny squares. Green squares are good chips; gray squares are
failed chips. Sasa Kun sneezes, one green square turns gray, and the robot
immediately deploys a tiny cleanroom umbrella.
